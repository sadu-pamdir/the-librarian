# Unsupported: "Chakras exist as physically measurable anatomical structures"

As an **empirical measurement claim**, this is unsupported: no anatomical or
physiological correlate of chakras has been demonstrated. Note the status is
`unsupported`, not `refuted` — absence of evidence is documented, a formal
disproof is not the right category here.

**This entry is one half of a complement pair.** The same word — "chakras" —
carries a second, different claim: an experiential map used coherently across
several traditions. That claim is of type `interpretive`, is *not* refutable
by anatomy, and lives in the positive wing:
[chakras-experiential-map](../chakras-experiential-map.md).

One catalog, two claims, two honest answers. Claims may only be judged
within their own claim type — and a claim that prescribes action in the
physical world is always held to the empirical standard (anti-abuse rule).
