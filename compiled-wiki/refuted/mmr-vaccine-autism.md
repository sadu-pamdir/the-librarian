# Refuted: "The MMR vaccine causes autism"

The claim originates from a 1998 Lancet paper by Andrew Wakefield et al.
(12 children, no controls). The Lancet issued a **full retraction in
February 2010** after the UK General Medical Council found serious
professional misconduct including dishonesty; Wakefield was struck off the
UK medical register. Large epidemiological studies and the Taylor et al.
2014 meta-analysis covering more than 1.2 million children find **no
association** between the MMR vaccine and autism.

This entry is the canonical example of why the refuted wing exists: the
retraction is itself hard-won knowledge. A library that only holds the true
loses the refutation — and with it the ability to answer this question at
all, leaving the field to the original falsehood.
