# Chakras as an experiential map (interpretive claim)

The chakra system describes a **map of subjective experience** — a
first-person vocabulary for locating attention, emotion and energy in the
body. As an interpretive framework it is judged by coherence and usefulness,
not by anatomical measurement: a map is not the territory.

Several traditions carry structurally similar maps (Indian yoga's chakras,
Tibetan tantra's channel-wheels, the Sufi lataif). This convergence is a
**prioritization signal** — "worth looking here" — not proof. The status is
therefore `attested`, deliberately weaker than `supported`.

**Complement pair:** the empirical claim "chakras exist as measurable
anatomical structures" is a different claim under the same word, and it is
unsupported: [refuted/chakras-as-measurable-anatomy](refuted/chakras-as-measurable-anatomy.md).
A missing empirical half does not invalidate the interpretive half — it
marks where the map still waits for territory.
